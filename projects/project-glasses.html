Augmented Reality: Glasses for Visually Impaired

Task

These glasses were built to give visually-impaired users more information about their surroundings by relaying distances via sound or visual text. They employed Arduino Nano, PETG 3D prints, ultrasonic distance sensors, and piezoelectric speakers along with a switchable power source. My iterative designs included laser-cut prototypes, microcontroller programming, and peer critical feedback. Using MIT App Inventor, I created an app that connects to Arduino Nano via Bluetooth to relay visual distance messages from the glasses to your phone.

Future Implementations

One of the next steps in creating a wearable devices is comfort and polish. While the device is easy to wear and the weight manageable, it does not possess the same sleekness as a normal pair of glasses or symmetry. Also, I could try to use the phone's built-in gyroscope, which has the capacity to enhance the glasses's sensitivity to its surroundings.
