Web Dev: Avicenna Hospital

Task

In collaboration with two other Brown University students, this website was built for our client, the #Sustainable International Medical Relief Organization (SIMRO), a Turkish based NGO founded by Syrian doctors with the purpose of rebuilding the Syrian Healthcare system. The site publicized new information regarding the hospital from CNN, the Guardian, and BBC, received donations, aggregated donation and donor data, and kept donors informed on current news online and via email subscription.

Context

The Syrian healthcare system, hospitals, and humanitarians acting to improve health, have been under attack as a form of warfare in the Syrian civil war. SIMRO Founder and CEO Dr. Khaled Almilaji, a former Brown MPH student until his student visa was revoked during the Trump Travel Ban, has spearheaded various SIMRO health care programs throughout Syria—the most recent being the construction and opening of the Avicenna Women’s & Children Hospital, the largest underground hospital in Idlib, Syria. Due to the ever-growing instability in Syria, it has been challenging to secure funding to fortify the structure, equip the hospital, secure salaries, and furnish the units. Therefore, SIMRO has relied on a grassroots donation structure to maintain the hospital. Our website is purported to aid activism about the Syrian civil war and spread awareness.
